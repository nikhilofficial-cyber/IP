## AI (ARITIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE)
- Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the branch of computer science that enables computers and machines to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, problem-solving, decision-making, and understanding language. ex :- Alexa , Siri , etc.

<br>

## MACHINE LEARNING (ML)
- Machine Learning (ML) is a branch (subset) of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that enables computers to learn from data and improve their performance without being explicitly programmed. ex :- YouTube recommends videos based on what you watch, Gmail identifies spam emails, etc .

<br>

## NATURAL LANGAUAGE PROCESSING (NLP)
- Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that enables computers to understand, interpret, process, and generate human language, such as English, Hindi, and other languages. ex:- Google Translate converts one language into another.

<br> 

## IMMERSIVE EXPERIENCES
- Immersive Experiences are interactive digital environments that make users feel as if they are part of a virtual or augmented world by using technologies such as Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR).

## Types of Immersive Experiences
### VIRTUAL REALITY (VR)
- Virtual Reality is a three-dimensional, computer-generated situation that simluates the real world. At present, it is achieved with the help of VR Headsets.

### Augmented Reality (AR)
- Augmented Reality (AR) is a technology that adds digital objects or information to the real world using smartphones, tablets, or AR glasses. ex: Pokémon GO, Snapchat filters, etc.
                OR
- The super-imposition of computer generated perceptual information over the existing physical surroundings is called as Augmented Reality (AR). ex: Pokémon GO, Snapchat filters, etc.

## BIG DATA
- Big Data is a large and complex collection of data that is difficult to store, process, and analyze using traditional data processing tools.

### Characteristics of Big Data (The 5 V's)
### 1. Volume
- The most prominent characteristic of big data is its enormous size. If a particular datasets is of such large size that is difficult to process it with traditional DBMS tools, it can be termed as big data.

### 2. Velocity
- It represents the rate at which the data under consideration are being generated and stored. Big data has an exponentially higher rate of generation than traditional data sets.

### 3. Variety
- It asserts that a dataset has varied data, such as structured, semi-structured and unstructured data. Some examples are text, images, videos, web-pages and so on.

### 4. Veracity
- Big data can be sometimes inconsistent, biased, noisy or there can be abnormality in the data or issues with the data collection methods. Veracity refers to the trust worthiness of the data because processing such incorrect data can give wrong results or mislead the interpretations.

### 5. Value
- Big data is not only just a big pile of data, but also possess to have hidden patterns and useful knowledge which can be of high business value . But as there is cost of investment of resources in processing big data, we should make a premliminary enquriy to see the potential of the big data in terms of value discovery or else our efforts could be in vain.

## INTERNET OF THINGS (IoT)
- Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of physical devices such as sensors, appliances, vehicles, and machines that are connected to the Internet. These devices collect, share, and exchange data automatically, allowing them to be monitored and controlled remotely with little or no human intervention.


### 1. WEB OF THINGS (WoT)
- Web of Things (WoT) is a technology that uses web standards and technologies (such as HTTP, URLs, and APIs) to connect and access Internet of Things (IoT) devices. It allows different smart devices to communicate and share data easily through the Web, making them more interoperable.


### 2. Sensors
- A sensor is an electronic device that detects or measures physical changes in the environment, such as temperature, light, pressure, motion, or humidity, and converts them into electrical signals or data for processing by a computer or other device.


### 3. SMART CITIES
- A Smart City is a city that uses Information and Communication Technology (ICT), Internet of Things (IoT), sensors, and data analytics to improve public services, manage resources efficiently, and enhance the quality of life of its citizens.

## CLOUD COMPUTING
- Cloud Computing is the delivery of computing services such as storage, servers, software, databases, and networking over the Internet, allowing users to store, access, and process data online instead of using a local computer or hard drive.

### CLOUD SERVICES
- Cloud Services are computing services provided over the Internet that allow users to access storage, software, servers, databases, and other IT resources without installing or managing them on their own computer.

### A. INFRASTRUCTURE AS A SERVICE (IaaS)
- Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) is a cloud service model that provides virtual computing infrastructure, such as servers, storage, networking, and virtual machines, over the Internet. Users manage the operating system and applications, while the cloud provider manages the hardware.

### B. PLATFORM AS A SERVICE (PaaS)
- Platform as a Service (PaaS) is a cloud service model that provides a platform with tools, operating system, and development environment for building, testing, and deploying applications without managing the underlying hardware.

### C. SOFTWARE AS A SERVICE (SaaS)
- Software as a Service (SaaS) is a cloud service model that provides ready-to-use software applications over the Internet. Users can access the software through a web browser without installing it on their computer.


|IaaS|PaaS|SaaS|
|----|----|----|
|Provides Infrastructure (servers, storage, networking).|Provides a Platform to develop applications.|Provides Software ready to use.|
|Used by System Administrators/IT professionals.|Used by Developers.|Used by End Users.|
|User manages OS and applications.|User manages only the applications they develop.|User only uses the software.|

## GRID COMPUTING
- Grid Computing is a type of computing in which multiple computers connected through a network work together as a single system to solve large and complex problems by sharing their processing power and resources. 
### GRID COMPUTING can be of two types :-
- **1. Data Grid** :- Data Grid , used to manage large and distributed data having required multi-user access 
- **2. CPU or Processor Grid** :- Where processing is moved from one PC to another as needed or a larger task is divided into subtasks and divided to various nodes for parallel processing.

## BLOCKCHAINS
- Blockchain is a distributed digital ledger that stores data in the form of blocks. These blocks are linked together in chronological order using cryptography, making the stored data secure, transparent, and difficult to modify.
